Climate and Disaster Resilient Agriculture

About CoE CDRA

Climate change induced increasing temperatures and heatwaves lead to changes in rainfall patterns, severe and frequent storms and increased drought. Future climate projections are anticipated to adversely affect biodiversity, crop yields and food security worldwide...

Objectives

  • Understand climatic risk and vulnerability in agriculture under current and future climate.
  • Build profitable and sustainable food systems using CSA technologies for Southern India.
  • Enhance capacity for implementing and scaling CSA among farmers and agencies.
  • Strengthen science-policy interface with evidence-based information.

Work Plan

The proposal includes climate risk assessments, sustainable food systems, stakeholder capacity building, and science-policy linkage.
